Kanniyakumari
Thiruvalluvar Statue
The Thiruvalluvar Statue is a 41-metre-tallstone sculpture of the Tamil poet and philosopher Valluvar, author of the Tirukkural.
Vivekananda Rock Memorial
In 1970, Swami Vivekananda was reported to have reached illumination atop the stone, and it was constructed in his honour.
Government Museum
The Government Museum exhibits stupefying wooden sculptures, botanical specimens, tribal items, bronzes, antique coins, zoological specimens and so on.
Mahatma Gandhi Mandapam
Mahatma Gandhi Memorial was built in the honour of Mahatma Gandhi, the father of our nation.
View Tower
The building is constructed in a circular shape, featuring five storeys. The most important landmarks of Kanyakumari can be spotted from here.
Mathoor Aqueduct
The aqueduct is built across the Parali river, a small river that originates in the Mahendragiri, Tamil Nadu hills of the Western Ghats.
Our Lady of Ransom Shrine
A brilliant example of Gothic architecture, the church was built during the 15th century and looks breathtakingly beautiful against the blue lashing waves of ocean behind it.
Tsunami Memorial Park
The Tsunami Monument is a memorial to the people in Kanyakumari who lost their lives due to the Tsunami of 26 Dec 2004.
